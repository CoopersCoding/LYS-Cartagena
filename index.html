About The Event

Embark on the adventure of a lifetime aboard Royal Caribbean's magnificent Utopia of the Seas for an extraordinary New Year's celebration in 2026. Your journey begins at Port Canaveral, Florida, where you'll set sail for crystal-clear waters and tropical paradises. Discover the vibrant culture and stunning beaches of Nassau, Bahamas, where turquoise waves lap against pristine shores and local treasures await your exploration. Next, experience pure bliss at Perfect Day at CocoCay, Royal Caribbean's private island paradise, where you can choose between heart-pumping thrills or ultimate relaxation in exclusive hideaways. Rejuvenate your spirit during a luxurious day at sea, where endless ocean views and world-class amenities create the perfect backdrop for making unforgettable memories. This spectacular five-day escape concludes with your return to Port Canaveral, leaving you with a treasure trove of experiences to cherish long after you've returned home.

Please Note: This Royal Caribbean cruise to the Bahamas & Perfect Day is a casual group trip, not a formal mixer event. While Last Year Single staff won't be present, this is a fantastic opportunity for members of our LYS community to connect and enjoy a vacation together! Once you register, we'll help you connect with other LYS members who are joining the cruise through a dedicated group. This is a casual group trip, not a formal mixer event.

  • What is the expected weather?

    Important Weather Information for Your Upcoming Cruise

    As your cruise departure approaches, we recommend keeping an eye on the weather forecast for both your departure city and cruise destinations. This will help you:

    • Pack appropriate clothing and accessories for the expected conditions
    • Plan any pre-cruise travel arrangements accordingly
    • Prepare for any potential weather-related schedule adjustments

    Recommended Weather Resources:

    • National Weather Service for US locations
    • AccuWeather.com for international destinations
    • Your cruise line's mobile app for ship-specific updates

    We suggest checking the forecast regularly starting about 10 days before departure, with more frequent checks in the final 3-4 days before the cruise. Remember that marine weather can be quite different from land conditions, and forecasts become more accurate as your departure date gets closer. The cruise line will contact you directly if there are any weather-related changes to your itinerary.
    Safe travels and smooth sailing!

  • Do I need travel insurance?

    While travel insurance is strongly encouraged for your cruise vacation, the choice is ultimately yours. It can protect your investment if unexpected illness, weather, or other events impact your plans. Many cruise lines offer their own insurance, but you're free to compare third-party options as well. The peace of mind often outweighs the additional cost, especially given the upfront investment a cruise requires.
